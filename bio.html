A longer, more informal bio

Before undergrad (-2023)

I've always been interested in science and math. In middle school, I did mathematics and computational linguistics olympiads. I had some decent success, but my interest pivoted to robotics in high school.

I joined my school's VEX robotics team in my freshman year of high school where I learned the fundamentals of robot mechanics, control, and design. Frankly, I was pretty terrible at it, but I was fortunate to be mentored by Jacob Zietek and Lily Coffin, who were seniors at the time. Here's some of the stuff I did in my time doing VEX:

  • Competed at Worlds (twice!)
  • Won a few regional competitions
  • Peaked 1st in NY and 30th in the world at some point
  • Co-created the VEX CAD Fusion 360 Library 2.0 with Gilbert Chang
  • Used classical computer vision techniques to automate disc throwing
  • Messed with control algorithms for faster autonomous routines
  • Led an awesome team for 3 years and designed+built+programmed several robots end-to-end
  • Founded CTOW, an alliance of elite teams across the world with 300+ awards to date including several state champion, national champion, Worlds division & overall champion titles
  • Met some amazing friends :)

I did a lot of Taekwondo before my undergrad as well (12 years of it :o). I'm a third degree black belt, and I taught classes for 5 years. I credit Taekwondo for a lot of the way I live my life and my work ethic. I learned a lot about discipline, hard work, and perseverance through it. I really encourage anyone who's curious to give it a shot!
